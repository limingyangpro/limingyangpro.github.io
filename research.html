Research

1. Point Pattern Matching & Recognition

Point is the most basic element in the world and thus point pattern can be found almost everywhere. Point pattern can be used for matching different models and for model recognition. For the generality purpose, we consider only "pure" point pattern, i.e. only point positions for matching and recognition tasks. Here are two examples:

A) Tracking diverse targets

Point sets automatically detected (marked in blue) are used. Different targets are pre-registered into the system, and they are then tracked by the system in real-time. Model position is marked by a red rectangle.


B) Augmenting engineering drawings

Line intersections detected in real-time (marked in blue) are used as point patterns. They are registered in real-time with pre-registered intersections, so that a pre-loaded 3D model can be overlaid onto the drawing.


Executable and code!!

  • A small demo in windows can be downloaded here.
  • You can also compile yourself in Linux by going to my Git.

Related papers:

L. Yang, J. M. Normand and G. Moreau, "Local Geometric Consensus: A General Purpose Point Pattern-Based Tracking Algorithm," in IEEE Transactions on Visualization and Computer Graphics, vol. 21, no. 11, pp. 1299-1308, Nov. 15 2015.

2. Augmented maps

How to geo-reference a paper map? Namely, decide its GPS position for given pixel on the map? We need to register between information from Geographic Information System (GIS) and paper maps! It can be down by hand, but better automatically!
This can be down by point pattern matching and here is an example video. Road network in green extracted from GIS are automatically overlaid onto paper maps.


Related papers:

Liming Yang, Jean-Marie Normand, and Guillaume Moreau. "Augmenting off-the-shelf paper maps using intersection detection and geographical information systems." IEEE International Conference on Machine Vision Applications (MVA), 2015 14th IAPR. 2015.

Liming Yang, Jean-Marie Normand, and Guillaume Moreau. "Robust random dot markers: towards augmented unprepared maps with pure geographic features." Proceedings of the 20th ACM Symposium on Virtual Reality Software and Technology (VRST). ACM, 2014.

3. Projector-Camera Calibration

A practical method for projector-camera system calibration by using random dots calibration patterns: the user only needs to manipulate a small calibration board (B4 size) for 30s to get precise and repeatable calibration results. It can be useful for large scale Spatial Augmented Reality (SAR).


Code:

  •  The code is available in Linux (it needs the library of LGC) in Github

Related paper:

Liming Yang, Jean-Marie Normand, and Guillaume Moreau. "Practical and precise projector-camera calibration." In Proceedings of the 2016 IEEE International Symposium on Mixed and Augmented Reality (ISMAR 2016).

4. Geometric reconstruction of Surface of Revolution (SoR) in dense SLAM

To understand the structure of a SLAM map, one needs to reconstruct primitives in first step. SoR can represent lots of objects and we show that they can be reconstructed in real-time.

Related Paper:

Liming Yang, Hideaki Uchiyama, Jean-Marie Normand, Guillaume Moreau, Hajime Nagahara and Rin-ichiro Taniguchi. "Real-time surface of revolution reconstruction on dense SLAM." In IEEE International Conference on 3D Vision (3DV 2016).
