About me

Liming YANG Ph.D

My research interests are real-time Computer Vision algorithms and applications, including Augmented Reality, Multi-modal registration, 3D reconstruction, Geometric Modeling, Projector-Camera Calibrations, etc. I am especially interested in multimodal registration based on geometric information.

During my Ph.D, I was working on fast and robust point pattern matching algorithms for registration and tracking, where only point positions are taken into considerations (local image patches, color or brightness are not used). A linear-behavior algorithm Local Geometric Consensus is proposed with respective to number of points (20-30 points/ms). Applications are developed by using this algorithm, such as augmented maps, augmented engineering drawings and projector-camera calibrations. As point is most basic element, the algorithm can be used in other domains.

I was also working on geometric reconstruction on SLAM map (Surface of Revolution reconstruction) at the end of my Ph.D. The dissertation can be downloaded here.

Besides, I am interested in theoretical physics, mechanics, economics, and I like doing sports.
